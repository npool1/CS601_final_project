This is a great way to make a quick, week night dinner that tastes like you spent all day in the kitchen. If you don’t have a pressure cooker, go out immediately and buy one! (We love our Fagor model.) But if you don’t have a Bed Bath & Beyond nearby, this recipe will still work in a regular, thick-bottomed stew pot/Dutch oven (you’ll just have to elongate the cooking time by a few hours).

Ingredients

  • 1/2-1lb of lamb stew meat cut into 1.5 inch chunks
  • 2 tbsp of olive oil (just enough to coat the bottom of pressure cooker and keep lamb from sticking and burning to bottom)
  • 2 tbsp all-purpose flour
  • 1 onion medium dice
  • 2 celery stalks medium dice
  • 1 large carrot medium dice
  • 2 cloves of garlic minced
  • 3 springs of rosemary
  • ¼ tsp red pepper flakes
  • ¾ cup red wine
  • 2 tsp Worcestershire sauce
  • 5 cups chicken stock
  • ½ lb fingerling potatoes rinsed and chopped
  • ½ bunch of Kale chopped 1 cup of
  • peas salt and pepper

Directions

  1. Add olive oil to pressure cooker and heat on medium high until shimmering.
  2. Season lamb with salt and pepper, then coat with a few tablespoons of all-purpose flour.
  3. Add coated meat to pan and brown on all sides.
  4. Remove browned lamb meat and place in bowl.
  5. Add onion, celery, and carrot to the pressure cooker. Season with a pinch of salt and pepper and sauté until tender.
  6. Add the minced garlic, 2 sprigs of rosemary, and red chili flakes. Cook until fragrant (about 30 secs).
  7. Add red wine and Worcestershire sauce. Cook this down until the pan is mostly dry and all of the alcohol has cooked off.
  8. Add the stock and increase the heat and bring to a boil.
  9. Add chopped potatoes and browned lamb (along with any juices that have accumulated in the bowl with the lamb).
  10. Seal and pressurize the pot.
  11. Once the pot is under pressure, reduce the heat to medium low and cook for 15 minutes.
  12. Depressurize the pot. (Be careful! The contents will be very hot and the pot itself will put out a lot of steam while it depressurizes.)
  13. Depressurize the pot. (Be careful! The contents will be very hot and the pot itself will put out a lot of steam while it depressurizes.)
  14. Add the rest of your vegetables and the last sprig of rosemary and season to taste with salt and pepper.
  15. Ladle into bowls and enjoy this one pot wonder!
